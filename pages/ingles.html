"What is the difference between INNER JOIN and LEFT JOIN?"
An INNER JOIN returns only the rows that have matching values in both tables. A LEFT JOIN returns all rows from the left table plus the matching rows from the right table — unmatched rows on the right appear as NULL. I use LEFT JOINs when I need to preserve all records from the primary table, for example when analyzing all customers including those who haven't made a purchase yet.
Técnica + cuándo usarlo = respuesta completa
"How do you handle missing data?"
My approach depends on the context. First, I profile the missingness: is it random, or is there a pattern? If missing values represent less than 5% of rows and appear random, I consider removing them. For numerical columns with higher missingness, I evaluate imputation strategies — mean or median for symmetric distributions, mode for categorical data, or KNN imputation when relationships between variables are strong. I always document the decision and validate that the post-imputation distribution is consistent with the original data.
Muestra proceso estructurado · no solo "elimino los nulos"
